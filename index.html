Analysis & Discussion

Performance Convergence: According to the charts, Q-learning learns a policy that is optimal in the deterministic sense (reward ~ -13) but suffers highly negative convergence during training (reward ~ -45) due to its $\epsilon$-greedy exploration causing it to step off the cliff constantly. Sarsa, taking the exploration policy into account, learns a longer but safer path, leading to better training performance (reward ~ -25).

Greedy Policy Paths: The final extracted policies (arrows above) demonstrate this tradeoff. The top Q-learning grid directs the agent strictly across the edge row right above the cliff. The bottom SARSA grid directs the agent to safely route one row upwards, preventing fatal mistakes during exploration.
